# Evaluation of 5-Day K1 Games from a 3-Year-Old's Perspective

## Day 1: BoBo 的世界 (BoBo's World) — Rating: 8/10
### What Works Well for Children:
- Colorful and cheerful visuals with a friendly panda character.
- Large, clickable buttons designed for small fingers.
- Simple prompts that engage the child without requiring reading skills.

### Needs Improvement:
- No audio guidance to accompany text prompts.
- Limited interactive elements. Could include more diverse activities beyond greetings.

### Top 3 Potential Issues:
1. Lack of audio support may make it challenging for non-readers.
2. Repeated gameplay may become unengaging after a while due to lack of variety.
3. Instructions displayed in text only.

### Recommendations:
- Add voice guidance for text instructions.
- Provide some interactive elements for the children (e.g., BoBo asking for a high-five).
- Create more stages or scenarios for extended involvement.

---

## Day 2: 太陽公公塗黃色 (Mr. Sun Paints Yellow) — Rating: 9/10
### What Works Well for Children:
- Interactive task (finding objects) is engaging and encourages cognitive skills.
- Vivid and clear design with a joyful color scheme.
- Immediate feedback with a scoring system encourages further play.

### Needs Improvement:
- Audio feedback for selected items would enhance the experience.
- Limited variety in tasks. Extending the number of rounds or increasing complexity slightly may enrich the experience.

### Top 3 Potential Issues:
1. No audio feedback when choosing an object.
2. Limited task means the game might be too short for some children.
3. Missing detailed celebrations for task completion.

### Recommendations:
- Introduce voice narration and celebratory animations upon task completion.
- Add more rounds with varying levels of difficulty.

---

## Day 3: 海底世界搵藍色 (Underwater World - Find Blue) — Rating: 9/10
### What Works Well for Children:
- The underwater theme is captivating and delightful.
- Clear instructions with an easy-to-read scoreboard.
- Visually distinct icons simplify understanding for young players.

### Needs Improvement:
- No audio guidance or sound effects when selecting objects.
- Gameplay is very similar to Day 2, which may diminish excitement for children.

### Top 3 Potential Issues:
1. Lack of motivating audio prompts or accompanying sound.
2. Gameplay repetition might reduce long-term engagement.
3. Mis-clicking could cause mild frustration if there’s no explanation.

### Recommendations:
- Provide positive audio feedback on successful input.
- Diversify tasks for novelty in subsequent days.

---

## Day 4: 果園數數 (Counting in the Orchard) — Rating: 8/10
### What Works Well for Children:
- Fun and educational number-based task aligning with K1 curriculum.
- Bright orchard visuals with engaging child-friendly elements.
- Maintains a clear, uncluttered interface.

### Needs Improvement:
- Audio instructions are missing, which could benefit toddlers who cannot read yet.
- More elaboration on the goal would help children focus better.

### Top 3 Potential Issues:
1. Non-readers might find it difficult to follow text-based instructions.
2. The game lacks celebratory animations or motivating rewards beyond numbers.
3. Limited interactions could make the activity short-lived for engagement.

### Recommendations:
- Add playful animations for both correct and incorrect answers.
- Incorporate voice/visual prompts for better understanding of the task.
